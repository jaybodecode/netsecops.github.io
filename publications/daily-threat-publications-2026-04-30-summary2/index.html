Summary

A critical day in cybersecurity for April 30, 2026, marked by the disclosure of 'Copy Fail' (CVE-2026-31431), a severe Linux kernel flaw allowing instant root access, and active zero-click exploitation of a Windows Shell vulnerability (CVE-2026-32202) for credential theft. A critical RCE in GitHub (CVE-2026-3854) also came to light, while the ShinyHunters group claimed responsibility for major data breaches at Medtronic and Udemy. Concurrently, reports from Europol and Fortinet highlighted the growing industrialization of cybercrime fueled by AI, leading to a surge in ransomware attacks.
