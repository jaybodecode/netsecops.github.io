Summary

This week in cybersecurity, Microsoft released a massive Patch Tuesday addressing over 130 vulnerabilities, while separately, two new Windows zero-days were dropped publicly by a disgruntled researcher. The supply chain remains a key target, with the 'Mini Shai-Hulud' campaign compromising over 170 open-source packages and the Nitrogen ransomware group hitting electronics giant Foxconn. Other major incidents include a critical Linux kernel flaw 'Fragnesia' allowing root access, and the EU's NIS2 Directive imposing strict new liability rules on the logistics sector. These events highlight the increasing speed and sophistication of threats, from rapid vulnerability exploitation to novel attack techniques.
