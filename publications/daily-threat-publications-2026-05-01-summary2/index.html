Summary

This week in cybersecurity is marked by a series of high-impact events, including the "Mini Shai-Hulud" supply chain attack compromising SAP, PyTorch, and Intercom packages, affecting over 1,800 developers. Critical zero-day vulnerabilities were disclosed, with "Copy.Fail" (CVE-2026-31431) offering instant root access on most Linux systems, and a cPanel auth bypass (CVE-2026-41940) exploited for months. Reports from Microsoft and Fortinet highlight alarming trends, with QR code phishing attacks surging 146% and AI-enabled tools contributing to a 389% increase in ransomware victims. CISA has issued an urgent directive to patch a Windows NTLM hash leak flaw now under active exploitation.
