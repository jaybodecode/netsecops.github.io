Massive Canvas Breach Hits 275M Users; Critical Zero-Days in Linux, cPanel, and PAN-OS Under Active Attack

Publication Date: May 10, 2026

Summary

This edition covers a catastrophic data breach at the education platform Canvas, claimed by ShinyHunters to affect 275 million users globally. The cybersecurity landscape is further shaken by the disclosure and active exploitation of several critical zero-day vulnerabilities, including the "Dirty Frag" flaw (CVE-2026-43284) granting root access on Linux systems, a PAN-OS bug (CVE-2026-0300) used by state-sponsored actors, and a cPanel vulnerability (CVE-2026-41940) leading to widespread ransomware and botnet infections. Supply chain attacks also remain a major threat, with incidents targeting the JDownloader website, the Braintrust AI platform, and a malicious repository on Hugging Face impersonating OpenAI.
